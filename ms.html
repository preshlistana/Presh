How might we design and implement a cost-effective hackathon strategy to increase brand presence and yield student application drafts?

Process

Make School is a college that offers a Bachelor‚Äôs in Applied Computer Science in 2-2.5 years with no upfront tuition required. Before joining the team, they hosted and participated in numerous hackathons. The strategy and implementation of this initiative was not properly documented and streamlined when I joined. Given that the high school hackathon community is one of our prime target markets, it was essential for us to have a clear strategy in how we connected and engaged with these students.

I took the time to learn what worked in the past and found that we spent thousands of dollars in hackathon sponsorships for speaking engagements. There also was not a document outlining the strategy and how to implement it. I took these two observations into mind as I lead our hackathon initiatives to increase brand presence and yield student application drafts.

Solution

One of the biggest contributions I had for this project was streamlining our sponsorship process. For example, I made it a requirement for every organizer interested in our sponsorship to submit a sponsorship request form. Then, I developed a vetting process that looked into the ROI of each hackathon and our team‚Äôs bandwidth to support financially and in people-time to do a keynote, lead a workshop and/or table for Make School. If all the variables aligned, I would negotiate the financial sponsorship amount. With all of our venue-sponsored hackathons, I also worked with my student-leads to engage our students to volunteer and mentor the high school hackathon participants. This process led us to spend at an all-time low with a $6,000 budget and support 20+ hackathons which yielded 30 application drafts and 8 submitted applications to our college.
