How might we provide opportunities for undergrads to apply their skills and experiences to make a tangible impact?

Process

When I was a college sophomore, I was frustrated with how theoretical and impractical my classes were with my interests and passions. This changed when I joined the University Innovation Fellowship (UIF) with my friends. UIF brought students from all backgrounds that were committed in developing innovation and entrepreneurship in their own Universities using design thinking. My friends and I co-founded the Invention Corps of Berkeley at that time to address the gap in skills and social impact from our collegiate experience.

Solution

The Invention Corps of Berkeley is a student-led organization that teaches undergrads human-centered design to apply towards impact-driven projects. Our club connects diverse teams with organizations that are addressing problems related to poverty, health, society and the environment. For a semester, our teams collaborate with their mentors to address a challenge framed in a ‚Äúhow might we question‚Äù. To-date, we have worked on 33 projects from various industries. You can find examples of our recent projects here.

Outside of the projects, all of our members participate in weekly workshops to learn and develop their skills in design thinking, project management, teamwork, wireframing, prototyping, and etc. These are tangible skills they use on their projects throughout the semester. There are also social events that strengthen our camaraderie and community. Now, our alumni work in roles from Software Engineering to Project Management in many different industries including tech, education, health, biomedical and consulting.
