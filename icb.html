How might we provide human-centered design opportunities for undergrads to apply their skills and experiences to make a tangible impact?

Process

As a sophomore at UC Berkeley, I was frustrated with how theoretical and impractical my classes were with my interests and passions. I was grateful to be invited to be a part of the University Innovation Fellowship (UIF) to bring more innovation and entrepreneurship to our own campuses using design thinking. The program supported us in navigating resources within our own Universities and connecting us with other student leaders. At that time, my friends and I co-founded the Invention Corps of Berkeley to address the gap in skills and social impact from our collegiate experience.

Solution

The Invention Corps of Berkeley is a student-led organization that teaches undergraduate students the human-centered design process to create tangible solutions to poverty, society, health and the environment. Every semester, diverse teams of students collaborate with professors, entrepreneurs, professionals and change-makers alike to address real-world challenges framed in a ‚Äúhow might we‚Äù question.
